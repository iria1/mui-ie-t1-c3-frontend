ğŸš¨ What Is Cyberbullying?

Cyberbullying is when someone is mean or hurtful online - like sending nasty messages, spreading rumors, or sharing embarrassing pictures or videos. Some ways that people can use the internet to make someone feel bad or upset are:
  • Sending hurtful and harmful messages about them.
  • Sharing embarrassing photos and videos featuring them.
  • Spread rumors about them on the Internet.
  • Leaving them out online.
  • Using their identity to create fictitious accounts.
  • Tricking people into thinking you are someone else.

ğŸŒŸ Why We Care?

According to UNICEF and the UN SRSG on Violence Against Children (2019):
  • 1 in 3 children in 30 countries have experienced cyberbullying.
  • 1 in 5 children have missed school as a result.
  • Children aged 10-15 are especially vulnerable, with sensitive emotions.
Cyberbullying can lead to serious mental health issues, including anxiety, depression, and low self-esteem.
You're not alone. Lots of kids go through this. That's why ChildCyberCare is here - to help you learn, stay safe, and support each other.
